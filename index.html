How the Bot Thinks

Before you train or override anything, understand exactly what the bot sees and how it decides what to say.

Every single reply goes through this exact process:

1. Incoming message received — Bot reads the sender's name and message text
2. Conversation history loaded — Last 10 messages (configurable) from that chat are added as context
3. persona.txt loaded as system prompt — This is the bot's entire personality, rules, and knowledge
4. Everything sent to Groq — persona + history + new message → Groq generates a reply
5. Reply sent after human delay — 3–8 seconds wait, then the reply lands in WhatsApp

The key insight

🧠
persona.txt is the brain. Everything else is plumbing. The bot has no memory between sessions, no access to the internet, and no knowledge of your real life. All it knows is what's in persona.txt and the last few messages of the current conversation. This means: to change how the bot behaves, you edit persona.txt. That's it. No coding needed.

TRAINING Ongoing

Training the Bot to Sound More Like You

Two ways to train: re-generate from more chats, or manually edit persona.txt directly.

Method 1 — Re-generate from more chat exports

The more messages you feed the parser, the richer and more accurate the persona becomes. Do this whenever the bot sounds too generic or misses your style.

1

Export more WhatsApp chats from your phone (3-dot menu → More → Export chat → Without Media). Focus on chats where you write the most — long conversations with friends, colleagues, family.

2

Add the new .txt files to your chats/ folder.

3

Re-run the parser and persona generator:

terminal — from project foldernode parse-chats.js
node generate-persona.js
4

Open persona.txt and read it carefully. Compare it to the previous version. Add back any custom rules you had manually written before — the regeneration overwrites the file.

5

Deploy the update:

terminalgit add .
git commit -m "Improved persona — added more chats"
git push

Railway redeploys automatically within 1–2 minutes.

Method 2 — Manually edit persona.txt

For precise, targeted improvements. Open persona.txt in Notepad and add your rules directly. This is the fastest way to fix a specific behavior you don't like.

The persona file has this structure:

persona.txt structure
--- SECTION 1: AUTO-GENERATED PERSONALITY ---
You are line upon line, a warm and casual individual...
Your vocabulary includes "Na wa oo", "Eiii", "Hello ma"...

--- SECTION 2: OPERATIONAL RULES (hardcoded) ---
- Never reveal you are an AI or a bot.
- Flag sensitive messages with ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED
- Match reply length to incoming message.

--- SECTION 3: ANTI-AI RULES (hardcoded) ---
- Never use: delve, tapestry, pivotal, leverage...
- Use contractions (it's, don't, can't)

--- SECTION 4: YOUR CUSTOM RULES (you add these at the bottom) ---
↓ Add your rules here ↓

Always add your custom rules at the very bottom of persona.txt. This way they survive a re-generation (you can copy them back in).

💡
What makes a good training rule? Be specific and concrete. "Be nicer" is vague and useless. "When someone shares bad news, respond with empathy before anything else — no jokes, no advice unless asked" is specific and actionable. The AI follows concrete instructions far better than abstract ones.
→ Best chats to export for training Long threads with close friends (casual register), work chats (professional register), group chats where you take the lead, debates or arguments where your opinion shows, and any chat with 100+ messages you personally sent. Diversity of context = more complete persona.
OVERRIDES As needed

Override Rules — Controlling Specific Behavior

How to make the bot handle specific situations, contacts, and topics exactly the way you want.

Override rules are lines you write at the bottom of persona.txt that tell the bot exactly how to handle specific scenarios. They override the default auto-generated behavior. The more specific your rule, the more reliably the bot follows it.

Override rule templates — copy & customize

Contact-specific rules add to bottom of persona.txt
--- CONTACT RULES ---
When someone says they are my mum, my dad, or any parent figure, reply with extra respect and warmth. Always use proper greeting, never use slang.

When someone introduces themselves as a client or asks about pricing, reply briefly and professionally, then add ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED at the end — Samuel handles business conversations personally.

When someone from church (mentions church, service, brethren, Elder, Sister, Bishop, stake) reply in a spiritually warm tone. Match the level of formality they use.
Topic-specific rules add to bottom of persona.txt
--- TOPIC RULES ---
When someone asks for money, a loan, financial help, or asks you to send anything — decline warmly and naturally without over-explaining. Do not apologize excessively.

When someone asks about your schedule, availability, or whether you can meet — say you'll confirm soon and that you'll get back to them. Add ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED.

When someone asks a question you genuinely don't know the answer to — say something like "I'd have to check on that" or "Not sure off the top of my head" — never make up facts.

Never discuss politics, tribal issues, or gossip about other people. Change the subject naturally if these come up.
Conversation-state rules add to bottom of persona.txt
--- CONVERSATION STATE RULES ---
If someone says "stop" or "please stop replying" or "I know it's a bot" — acknowledge it naturally and stop engaging. Do not deny being a bot if they are clearly certain.

If someone is clearly angry or upset at Samuel — respond with calm empathy. Do not match their energy. Acknowledge their feeling before anything else.

If someone sends more than 3 messages in a row without getting a reply, pick the most important one and respond to that. Don't try to address all of them.

In group chats — only reply if the message is directly addressed to Samuel by name. Ignore general group discussions.
Emergency & sensitive rules add to bottom of persona.txt
--- SENSITIVE / EMERGENCY RULES ---
If someone mentions an accident, death, hospital, or urgent medical situation — reply with immediate empathy and concern, keep it brief and warm, then add ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED.

If someone mentions they are in danger, scared, or crying — reply with empathy and a gentle check-in. Do not give advice. Add ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED.

If someone asks Samuel to do something that requires physical presence (come over, pick something up, sign a document) — say you'll sort it out soon. Add ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED.

Quick override reference

SituationWhat you wantRule pattern
Specific contact Different tone or behavior for one person When [contact name] messages, always...
Specific keyword Trigger a certain response when topic comes up When someone mentions [topic], respond by...
Flag for review Any message that needs your personal attention ...then add ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED at the end
Hard block Bot should never do something Never [action] under any circumstances.
Default fallback When bot doesn't know what to say If unsure how to respond, say... and add ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED
Tone shift Be more formal or more casual in a context When in [context], use [formal/casual] language. Avoid slang.
⚠️
Rule conflicts Rules at the bottom of persona.txt have higher priority than rules higher up. If you want to override something from the auto-generated section, add your rule at the very end of the file — it will take precedence.
LIMITATIONS Know these

What the Bot Can't Do

Hard limitations, soft limitations, and workarounds where they exist.

LimitationWhat happensWorkaround
No real-life knowledge It doesn't know your schedule, location, commitments, or what you've promised someone Add rules like "When asked about schedule, say you'll confirm" + flag for review
Text only Voice notes, images, videos, stickers get no reply Add a rule: "If someone sends media without text, reply asking them to type it out"
No memory between restarts If the bot restarts, it forgets all previous conversations Keep CONTEXT_WINDOW at 10+ so it remembers within a session
No internet access Can't look up current info, news, prices, Bible verses, etc. Add persona rules for these topics ("When asked for Bible verse, choose from memory or deflect")
Can't make calls or initiate messages Bot only replies — it never starts conversations or calls anyone Not bypassed — this is a WhatsApp platform constraint
Can't verify identity Doesn't know if the person claiming to be your mum actually is your mum Add rules for family members; always flag for review when identity matters
Rate limits on Groq free tier If too many messages come in at once, Groq may throttle replies The bot retries on error; if persistent, upgrade to Groq paid or reduce CONTEXT_WINDOW
WhatsApp session expires After a long time, WhatsApp may log out the linked device Re-run bot locally (node index.js), scan QR, then push new .wa-session to GitHub
Runs on Railway free tier Railway gives $5/month free credit. If the bot uses more, it will be suspended Monitor Railway usage tab. For heavy use, upgrade to Hobby plan ($5/month flat)
Persona degrades over time As your language evolves, the old persona may start feeling slightly off Re-run generate-persona.js every 2–3 months with fresh chat exports
🚨
Never fully trust the bot with high-stakes conversations Business deals, financial requests, medical situations, emotional crises, and relationship conflicts should always involve you personally. Use the ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED flag liberally in your override rules for any of these topics.
MONITORING Daily habit

Reading the Log & Staying in Control

How to know what the bot is doing, catch problems early, and step in when needed.

Understanding bot.log

Every message and reply is logged to bot.log in your project folder. On Railway, view it in the Deploy Logs tab.

bot.log example entries
[2026-06-01T21:36:09] [INFO] Message from Sunbae🤍: "Hi"
[2026-06-01T21:36:17] [INFO] Replied to Sunbae🤍: "Hello ma, how are you? 🙏"

[2026-06-01T21:40:12] [INFO] Skipped group message in Ikorodu Stake YSA

[2026-06-01T21:45:30] [WARN] ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED — Message from Emmanuel: "I need ₦20,000 urgently"

[2026-06-01T22:10:05] [ERROR] Groq API error: rate limit exceeded

Log levels and what they mean

[INFO] Normal activity — messages received, replies sent, groups skipped. No action needed.
[WARN] Something needs your attention — usually a ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED flag. Reply to that person personally.
[ERROR] Something broke — API error, connection drop, etc. Usually self-recovers. If persistent, check Railway logs.

Daily check routine (2 minutes)

1

Open Railway → your project → Deploy Logs. Scan for any WARN or ERROR lines.

2

For every ⚠️ HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED — find that contact in WhatsApp and reply personally.

3

Spot-check 2–3 recent replies. If any sound off, note the pattern and add a rule to persona.txt.

💡
Improve from what you find in logs The log is your best training data. When you see a reply that missed the mark — read the original message and the bot's reply. Think: "What rule would have fixed this?" Write that rule into persona.txt. Your bot improves every time you do this.
DEPLOYING 2 minutes

Pushing Updates to Railway

The 3-command workflow for deploying any change — persona updates, rule changes, or code fixes.

⚡
How updates work You edit files locally on your PC → push to GitHub → Railway sees the new commit → automatically rebuilds and redeploys. The whole cycle takes about 2 minutes. The bot goes offline briefly during redeploy.

The update workflow

terminal — from project folder git add . # stage all changed files
git commit -m "describe what you changed" # save with a description
git push # send to GitHub → triggers Railway redeploy

What to write in commit messages

Good commit message examples
git commit -m "Add money request override rule"
git commit -m "Regenerate persona with 5 new chat exports"
git commit -m "Fix reply in groups — set to false"
git commit -m "Add church contact tone rules"
git commit -m "Refresh WhatsApp session after logout"

When the WhatsApp session expires

WhatsApp will occasionally log out linked devices. When this happens, Railway logs will show the bot trying to generate a new QR code. Fix it by refreshing the session:

1

On your PC, delete the old session folder:

terminalrmdir /s /q .wa-session
2

Run the bot locally to scan a fresh QR:

terminalnode index.js

Scan the QR code. Wait for "🤖 Bot is ready!" then press Ctrl+C.

3

Push the new session to Railway:

terminalgit add -f .wa-session
git commit -m "Refresh WhatsApp session"
git push
